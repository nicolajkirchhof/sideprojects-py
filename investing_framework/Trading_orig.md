## Rules

### Golden Trading Rules

- MANAGE BY RULES, NOT BY FEELINGS!!!
- No adjustments on trades until the time for the trade management interval is at least 80% done, e.g. for Options it's the last 1.5h before close.
- Don't exit a trade when your trade assumption hasn't been invalidated, but exit immediately when it is. AKA no early profit / loss taking based on PnL.
- Only fade a trend if you have a proven strategy!
- Otherwise NEVER fight the trend, not with options, not with underlyings NEVER
- NEVER add to a losing trade!!!
- NEVER trade something without defined rules!!!
- Exit short premium at 50D if you do not plan on assignment. Everything below is fastly bleeding your capital and should not be allowed.

### Portfolio management / Global rules

- Do not use more than 2% BP per Trade (max loss)
- Do not compound risk by assigning too much BP (> 30%) to one profit mechanism / sector / strategy => DIVERSIFY
- Hedge against Volatility events like 2024-08,2025-04 (TBD)
- Use uncorrelated assets from different markets like GC,ES,CL,ZN,ZB,6E,6J,Equities from different sectors, ...
- Use uncorrelated profit mechanisms e.g. directional, theta decay, volatility
- Log everything!
- Respect your timeframe! Log it along with the trade and stick to it.

## Profit Mechanism -- Momentum

### PEADs

**Hypothesis**

Stocks tend to follow the earnings surprise over the next quarter

**Research**

- How many stocks incorporate that behavior - momentum analysis dashboard
- Is it related to the gap on earnings?
- It's definitely related to the extend of the move, the company size, ...
- Is is related to the performance of the sector / market?

**Setup**

- Check earnings moves with momentum analysis dashboard
- What is the probability of a large move, this is less for larger companies
- Define trade based on probabilities

### Breakout Momentum

**Hypothesis**

Underlyings stay in consolidation from which they break out / down to new consolidation levels

Auction market theory

**Setup**

- Underlying has a trend, then a consolidation of at least a week two multiple weeks before it starts to move up and break out again
- Triggers are: earnings (separate PEADS), news, bubbles, sector rotation
- Breakout can be an opposite facing trendline (flag breakout) or a break of the last significant high
- The breakout is invalidated on a double close back into the breakout range

**Trades**

- Based on the strength of the breakout, volatility and market class of the underlying
- Short vol works great for larger underlyings that usually don't have extended moves
- OTM Calls/Puts work well for smaller underlyings where a move up to 100% is possible
- Mixtures work well for underlyings where a steady grind up is expected (long/short stock or synthetic long/short)

## Swing Trading Strategy -- Purely Momentum Based

### Trend following

**Behavior & Expectancy**

- Underlyings trade in ranges if there are no news and break out of ranges on new information that is significant
  - Earnings, Politics, Economics, ...
- Expectancy to be checked against analytics dashboard
  - ATR X => How big was the initial move
  - PEADS => How big was the last earnings surprise
  - News => Are there events that affect the bias on the company in the next days/weeks

**Indicators**

- Identify ranges of equilibrium based on TA
- Trendline break, Green line breakout, Major High/Low break
- Buy on breakout of a range, SL should be below the breakout point in the middle of the range
- Options trade duration should match the expectancy of the move some underlyings usually do short bursts, others can trade long term above MAs
- Default following: daily on 5 EMA exit trade on second close below it

**Trade Structure**

- If underlying has liquid options, use them to setup a directional structure based on your expectancy of the move
- Buy X amount stock based on your SL level and the expected move

### Stock selection criteria

- Sector Performance
- Not overextended (ATR)
- P/E
- No 3 days trend in direction
- VCP Volatility contraction in 3 PBs
- RS >=90%, holds if market down

| ATR | Trend Definition | Character of Move |
|---|---|---|
| 0 - 3 | Early/Stable Trend | The "Sweet Spot." The stock is moving up in sync with its average volatility. Very sustainable. |
| 3 - 6 | Established Trend | Strong momentum. The stock is "trending" clearly, but the risk-to-reward for new entries starts to diminish. |
| 7 - 10 | Extended Trend | The trend is reaching a climax. The price is moving faster than its historical volatility can justify. |
| > 10 | Parabolic/Exhausted | High probability of a "mean reversion" (price crashing back to the 50MA). |

### Trade management - Guidelines

- Look into the behavior of your playlist against your expectations and pre market moves in the first 15 min
- Set your stop limit entry and stop limit stop order. Limit is above 15 min / 30 min candle. SL is at the min of the day or ADRP20 whatever is smaller.
- Take partial profit after 1-2R, 4R, and what levels make sense
- Do not use the MAs for SL but rather follow the 'stair steps'
