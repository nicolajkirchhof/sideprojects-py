# Setup Reference — Visual Cues, Volume & Options

---

## ATR Extension Table

| ATR from base | Stage | Action |
|---------------|-------|--------|
| 0–3× | Early/Stable | Sweet spot — full size |
| 3–6× | Established | Diminishing R:R — normal size |
| 7–10× | Extended | Climax risk — half size or skip |
| >10× | Parabolic | High reversion risk — no entries |

## Type A — Episodic Pivot

**Why it works:** Institutional funds can't buy their full position in one day. The EP is
day one of 40–60 days of accumulation (PEAD research confirms the drift duration).

**Chart:** Large white gap at open. Solid green candle closing near its high. Volume bar
is the tallest on the chart — 5–10× the 50d average. Stock holds the gap intraday.
Closes in top 25% of day's range (Velez late-day validation = Big Money still buying).

**Entry:**
- 15min ORB: buy stop above 15min candle high. Use 15min (not 30min) — EP moves fast.
- Last-hour ORB: if stock in top 25% at 15:15, enter above first 15min last-hour candle.
- Day-after: 30min ORB next morning if EP day was too volatile; requires no gap reversal.
- MOC: if stock held above EP day open for full session — institutional "still buying" signal.

**Options:** Long ATM call 45–60 DTE (high gamma for PEAD drift). If IV spiked on EP:
bull call vertical instead (negative vega offsets IV spike).

**Volume validation:** RVOL > 2× by 9:45 = strong. RVOL < 1.5× by 10:00 = reduce size.
Heavy volume on a DOWN move during the day = distribution = do not enter.

**Skip if:** Below-avg volume gap · gap fills intraday · closes bottom 50% of range ·
ambiguous catalyst · ATR > 10 before the event.

## Type B — VCP Breakout

**Why it works:** Each pullback in the base shakes out weak holders. When the last seller
is gone, even modest buying drives an explosive move (supply exhaustion = line of least
resistance higher).

**Chart:** Series of shrinking pullbacks in a base (e.g. 20% → 12% → 6% → 3%).
Duration 2–6 weeks. Final 1–2 weeks: volume near multi-month lows (VDU).
Tightest days: high-to-low range ≤ 2%. RS line hits new highs before price does.
Breakout candle: 40–50%+ above avg volume, closes near high.

**Entry:**
- 30min ORB (preferred): pre-set buy stop above 30min candle high on breakout day.
- Cheat entry: Elephant Bar or Tail Bar near 10d EMA inside base → enter before main pivot.
  Tighter stop, better R:R.
- Never enter if close goes back inside the base — signal invalidated.

**Options:** Long ATM call 45–60 DTE. High gamma for the expansion move.
Cheat entry: long call when IVR is still low inside the base.

**Volume:** Base should trend quieter left-to-right. Breakout bar unambiguous spike.
Days 2–3 post-breakout: above-avg volume confirms. Below-avg on days 2–3 = fading.

**Skip if:** Breakout on below-avg volume · base < 5 days · ATR > 7 before base ·
earnings within 5 days · widening base (volume not contracting).

## Type C — EMA Reclaim / Wedge Pop

**Why it works:** Institutions add to winning positions on pullbacks to moving averages.
The reclaim signals their bid has appeared.

**EMA Crossback (Kell):** Stage 2 uptrend (10 > 20 > 50, all sloping up). Pullback to 10d
or 20d EMA on contracting volume. Reclaim candle snaps back above the EMA.

**Elephant Bar (Velez):** Candle ≥ 2× avg bar size. Opens near/below EMA, closes above it
near the high. Tail Bar variant: long lower wick stings the EMA and recovers.

**Wedge Pop (Kell):** Lower highs + lower lows within an uptrend form a wedge. Price snaps
above both 10 and 20 EMA simultaneously — the wedge is broken.

**EMA stack check before entry:**
- 10 > 20 > 50 all sloping up: full size ✓
- 10 > 20 but 50 flat: reduce size
- 20 < 50: trend broken — skip Type C

**Entry:**
- 15min ORB for large Elephant Bar; 30min for standard reclaim.
- Last-hour entry valid if stock reclaims EMA and holds top 25% of range into 15:15.

**Options:** Long ATM call 30–45 DTE (shorter than EP/VCP — smaller expected move).
Extended trend (>20 days running): PMCC — buy deep ITM call 90 DTE, sell OTM call 20 DTE.

**Skip if:** Volume expanding on pullback (distribution) · 2+ EMA fail attempts ·
NO-GO regime · stock > 20% above 10d EMA before pullback (still too extended).

## Volume Framework

| Phase | Healthy | Warning |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Base building | Contracting | Expanding = active sellers |
| VCP final days | Near multi-month lows | Any expansion |
| EP / Breakout | 5–10× avg (EP), 40–50%+ (VCP) | Below avg = no conviction |
| EMA reclaim | Expanding vs pullback | Contracting |
| Pullback to EMA | Contracting | Expanding = distribution |
| Up days in trend | Higher than down days | Lower than down days |

RVOL in real-time: >2.0 by 9:45 = full size · 1.5–2.0 = normal · <1.5 on breakout = skip

## Informed Options Flow

**Bullish adds to watchlist priority:**
- OTM call volume > 3× OI in next 4 weeks → directional bet being placed
- Block prints in multi-leg call spreads in final 2 hours → institutional footprint
- IV term structure steep: 6M IV >> 1M IV → market overstating long-term risk

**Caution signals:**
- OTM put volume > call volume (ratio > 70% puts) → reassess thesis

**Pre-earnings IV expansion:**
- IVR < 30% on stock that historically ramps IV into earnings
- Check: did IV expand 5–10 days before report in last 4 quarters?
- If yes: enter ATM call or call diagonal 14 days before earnings; exit 1 day before

## Common Mistakes

**Developing candle entry.** Entering before the 15/30min candle closes. The forming
candle can look like anything. Always wait for the close.

**Parabolic chase.** ATR > 10, stock "looks unstoppable." Highest reversion probability.
Buy after it bases for 3+ weeks — not while running.

**Low-volume breakout.** Volume "above average" but not convincingly. High false breakout
probability. Wait for last-hour ORB to confirm institutional follow-through.

**Triple EMA fail.** Stock touches 10/20 EMA twice+ without follow-through. Buyers
exhausted. Skip Type C — wait for a proper VCP base to form.

**Earnings gamble.** Entering a setup with earnings within 5 days. Binary event overrides
all technicals. Wait for the post-earnings reaction.

**Narrative overstay.** When the thesis appears on CNBC or Bloomberg, the information edge
is gone. Tighten the trailing stop — the drift is in its final stages.

**Regime ignorance.** All three setup types fail more in bear market conditions.
The regime filter is the highest-leverage decision in the system — it's not optional.
