## 01 -- Profit Mechanisms

What I trade · Why it works · How to identify it

### Mechanism 1 -- Breakout Momentum

**THE THESIS**

Momentum persists because institutions can't deploy capital instantly and information diffuses slowly. The ignition event forces large funds to start building. They buy for weeks -- not days.

**VCP Breakout (Minervini)**

Supply exhaustion -- each pullback shakes out weak holders. When the last seller is gone any buying drives price explosively. 2-6 weeks of tightening volatility; volume-confirmed break.

**EMA Reclaim (Kell + Velez)**

Institutions add to winning positions on pullbacks to moving averages. The reclaim candle signals their bid has re-appeared. Lowest risk entry, tightest stop.

**Episodic Pivot (Kullamagi + PEAD)**

Gap-up on 5-10x volume is day 1 of 40-60 days of institutional accumulation. Strong earnings beat amplifies the drift -- the bigger the gap and the stronger the beat, the longer the follow-through.

**PEAD -- EARNINGS AS MOMENTUM CATALYST**

PEAD is not a separate strategy -- it is a momentum amplifier. A strong earnings surprise creates 40-60 days of forced institutional accumulation. The gap is day 1, not the full trade.

- Gap size: >= 10% with top-25% close = strongest PEAD signal
- Beat magnitude: Larger EPS surprise = longer institutional follow-through
- Company size: Small/mid cap = bigger price drift; large cap = more muted
- Sector context: Strong sector confirms; weak sector diminishes effect

**HOW TO IDENTIFY -- 5-BOX CHECK**

| Box | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Regime | SPY/QQQ above 50d & 200d SMA · VIX < 20 · Breadth expanding |
| Stage 2 | Price > 50d > 200d · All EMAs sloping up · Within 25% of 52W high |
| RS | RS line at/near new highs vs SPY · Holds when market dips |
| Base / Catalyst | VDU in base or fresh gap with catalyst · ATR 0-6x · Not extended |
| Risk | Stop <= 7% · R:R >= 2:1 · Size = 0.5% portfolio risk |

Pre-earnings anticipation: RS stock + >=3 of 4 prior beats + holding 20d EMA at T-14 -> ATM call entry, IVR < 30%, exit day before report. Never hold through the binary event.

### How to Build Any New Profit Mechanism

Use this 4-step Outlier framework when researching a new mechanism. Do not trade it until all four steps are validated.

1. **Define the Profit Mechanism**
   Identify the SPECIFIC market effect you're trying to capture.
   - Price movement -- drift, momentum, breakout/down
   - Volatility -- expansion, contraction, mean reversion
   - Yields / correlations / structural alpha

2. **PM Behavior & Signals**
   Build tools to identify, measure, and time the mechanism.
   - Document behavior & conditions; assess severity & robustness
   - Build signal list -- test each signal against the PM
   - Match best signals; trim to the most predictive set

3. **Outline Fitting Structures**
   Identify how to monetise it via the right trade structure.
   - Create candidate structures; test performance per structure
   - Overlay portfolio risk rules and BP constraints
   - Identify the best-fit structure for this mechanism

4. **Build the Strategy**
   Turn the best structure into a fully defined, testable strategy.
   - Isolate key inputs -- option structure, greeks, sizing
   - Test rules and codify into a strategy card; assess portfolio fit
   - Backtest -> paper trade -> live test -> add to mechanism deck

### Technical Analysis -- Price Structure & Key Patterns

Al Brooks: markets are always in a trading range at some degree. Every breakout is just the start of a new range until proven otherwise. Context is everything.

**PRICE STRUCTURE**

- **Equilibrium & breakouts** -- Identify high-volume nodes (points of control) -- these are equilibrium zones. Breakouts from them that hold are the highest-probability continuation entries. Measure up and down legs to calibrate expected targets.
- **Multi-timeframe context** -- Weekly -> Daily -> 65min. Weekly sets the trend direction. Daily sets the setup. 65min sets the entry. Never fight the higher timeframe. Al Brooks: 'Always know the trend at least 2 timeframes up.'
- **Strong vs weak trend** -- Strong: candles getting bigger, gaps increasing, wicks small, volume rising on up days. Weak: same-size candles, wicks growing, volume declining. Trend strength determines position size and exit aggressiveness.
- **Consolidation cycle** -- Consolidation -> Breakout/Trend -> Consolidation -> (Counter) Breakout. The longer the consolidation the greater the potential move. Brooks: 'Never expect an immediate trend reversal without at least 2 legs.'
- **Two-legged pullbacks (Brooks)** -- After a trend leg, expect 2 legs down before resumption. First pullback is usually bought. Second pullback to a key level (EMA, prior support) is the high-probability entry. Don't chase leg 1.
- **52-week position + measured move** -- Stock within 10% of 52W high = no overhead supply. Prior base size predicts target: measure the depth of the prior base and add to the breakout point for a first target.

### Option Chain Analysis

Options are a magnifier, not a shortcut. Read the chain for information first, structure second. Never use options to avoid defining risk.

**LIQUIDITY CHECK (before anything else)**

- Bid/ask spread < $0.10 on the strikes you plan to trade
- Open interest > 500 on target strike and expiry
- Daily option volume confirming (not phantom OI)
- Use calls or debit spreads only -- no naked short on individual stocks

**DIRECTIONAL SIGNALS**

- OTM call volume > 3x OI in next 4W = informed institutional bid
- Block prints in multi-leg call spreads in final 2h = footprint
- IV term structure steep: 6M IV >> 1M IV = market overstating risk
- Put/call ratio on RS stock > 0.7 = potential squeeze setup

**IV CONTEXT**

- IVR < 40%: buy options (cheap premium = good for long calls)
- IVR 40-70%: debit spreads preferred (reduce vega exposure)
- IVR > 70%: no long options -- use defined spreads only
- IV expansion direction: Commodities = upside · Stocks = mixed · Indices = downside

**STRUCTURE SELECTION -- MATCHED TO YOUR SETUPS**

| Setup | Structure | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Type A -- EP | Long ATM call 45-60 DTE | IVR < 40%; if IV spiked on gap: use bull call vertical instead (negative vega offsets spike) |
| Type B -- VCP | Long ATM call 45-60 DTE or debit spread | Long call if IVR < 40%; bull call spread if IVR 40-70%; cheat entry: buy call when IVR still low inside base |
| Type C -- EMA Reclaim | Long ATM call 30-45 DTE or PMCC | Shorter DTE than EP -- smaller expected move. Extended trend > 20 days: PMCC (deep ITM 90 DTE + sell OTM 20 DTE) |
| Pre-earnings drift | ATM call or call diagonal | Entry at T-14, IVR < 30%. Call diagonal: buy later expiry, sell front-month OTM. Exit 1 day before earnings -- always. |

Options rule: close if option loses 50% of premium paid OR underlying breaks the stop level -- whichever comes first

### Fundamental Analysis -- Confirming the Catalyst

You are not a value investor. Fundamentals are used to confirm the catalyst is real and the institutional thesis is durable -- not to find cheap stocks.

**EARNINGS QUALITY**

- EPS beat magnitude: > 5% beat = meaningful, > 15% = strong PEAD candidate
- Revenue beat alongside EPS (cost-cutting alone = weak)
- Acceleration: are EPS and revenue growth rates increasing quarter over quarter?
- Consistency: beat >= 3 of last 4 quarters = credible follow-through expectation
- Gap size on last report vs this one -- is the market responding more strongly?

**VALUATION CONTEXT (FOR MOMENTUM)**

- P/E vs sector peers: not looking for 'cheap' -- avoiding severe overvaluation
- P/S ratio: growth stocks with P/S > 30x are vulnerable to derating
- Forward P/E trend: declining forward P/E + rising price = earnings growing faster than price
- Avoid: P/E > 100x with no clear near-term catalyst -- sentiment-only trade

**SHORT INTEREST & INSTITUTIONAL**

- Short interest > 15% of float = potential squeeze amplifier
- Days-to-cover > 5 = significant squeeze fuel if stock breaks out
- 13F changes: did funds ADD in the last quarter? = accumulation confirmation
- New institutional buyers with > 1% stake = conviction, not just quant flow
- Insider buying (SEC Form 4): management buying = strongest catalyst confirmation

**PEAD AMPLIFIERS**

- Large gap (> 15%) + top-quartile close + sector tailwind = strongest PEAD setup
- Small/mid cap (< $10B market cap) = larger price drift than large cap
- Sector in rotation: strong sector x strong stock = compounding momentum effect
- Prior PEAD history: did the stock drift after last 2-3 earnings beats? = pattern confirmation

### Key Learnings -- Y2

Hard-won lessons from real trades. Each represents a mistake made, recognised, and codified. Add new ones as they occur.

1. **Managing too soon creates losses** -- If a trade hasn't hit a defined premise (SL, range break), don't touch it. ORCL taught this twice -- leaving the initial trade would have prevented losses.
2. **The close is the only decision point** -- THE CLOSE COUNTS. Never make decisions based on a forming candle. Wait. The developing candle can look like anything.
3. **Never adapt a failing trade** -- Exit immediately when a premise is invalidated. 'Adapting' a losing trade is a lie you tell yourself. Close it. Re-enter on a clean setup.
4. **Never sell premium without IVP/IVR filter** -- Undefined premium only on large indices. Never naked premium on individual stocks unless comfortable with full assignment at that price.
5. **Always check BP before entry** -- ALWAYS look at buying power and maintenance before placing a trade. One forgotten BP check can break a month of good work.
6. **Each trade needs a pre-defined SL** -- Max SL as % of premium, DTE, or a specific technical level -- before entry. No thinking during the trade. Just execution.
7. **Respect your timeframe** -- You are not an intraday trader. Always set up trades that don't need intraday management. Emergency exit = stop-loss, not discretion.
8. **Small underlyings = best learning lab** -- Small stocks with liquid options provide the fastest feedback loop for learning trade management. Use them intentionally for skill-building.

### Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **RULES, NOT FEELINGS** -- Every decision is pre-defined. If you find yourself 'thinking' during a trade, you're already breaking this rule.
2. **THE CLOSE COUNTS** -- Never act on a developing candle. Wait for the close. Always. The forming candle can look like anything.
3. **NEVER ADD TO A LOSER** -- One stop = close the full position. Scaling into a losing trade multiplies mistakes, not recovery.
