## Trading Styles

## Oliver Velez: The "Institutional Footprint" Style

Summary: Velez identifies where "Big Money" is entering the market by watching how price interacts with the 20-period moving average. He treats the 20 SMA as a "magnet" and only trades in the direction of institutional flow.

**Strategy Details**

- **The Center of Gravity:** Price always returns to the 20 SMA. Velez avoids "buying high" by only entering trades when price is near or just bouncing off the 20 SMA.
- **The 20/200 Power Play:** The highest probability moves occur when a rising 20 SMA is above a rising 200 SMA. This indicates both short-term and long-term institutional alignment.
- **Core Stock Universe:** He recommends trading a small "family" of 10–20 liquid, large-cap stocks (e.g., NVDA, AAPL) to become deeply familiar with their price "personality."
- **Wealth Building Timeframe:** For 20–60 day swings, he uses Daily and Weekly charts together. A daily setup is only valid if the Weekly 20 SMA is also sloping upward.

**Execution Signals**

- **The Elephant Bar (EB):** A large, solid-bodied candle (at least 2x the average bar size). An "Igniting Green EB" off the 20 SMA is a primary entry signal.
- **The Tail Bar (TB):** A candlestick with a long lower wick that "stings" the 20 SMA and recovers. This proves that institutions stepped in to buy the dip.
- **The Bull 180:** A two-bar pattern where a red candle is immediately engulfed by a green candle. This "reclaiming of territory" marks a sharp reversal.
- **The 40/40/20 Rule:** Velez emphasizes that success is 40% entry location, 40% trade management (scaling out), and 20% mental discipline.

## Mark Minervini: The "SEPA® Momentum" Style

Summary: Minervini combines explosive fundamental growth with a specific technical pattern called the Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP) to find "Superperformers."

**Strategy Details**

- **The Trend Template:** A stock must be in a Stage 2 Uptrend: Price > 150 SMA > 200 SMA, with the 200 SMA trending up for at least a month.
- **VCP (Volatility Contraction):** He looks for the price to "tighten" from left to right. A typical pattern shows pullbacks shrinking from 25% to 10% to 3% as supply is absorbed.
- **Fundamental Acceleration:** Targets companies with 20%+ quarterly earnings and sales growth, preferably accelerating over the last 2-3 quarters.
- **Relative Strength Ranking:** Focuses on stocks with an IBD-style RS Rating of 90 or higher, meaning the stock is outperforming 90% of the market.

**Execution Signals**

- **The Pivot Point:** The exact price level where the "tightest" part of the VCP is broken on volume that is at least 40–50% above average.
- **The Low Cheat:** An entry point inside the base where the price clears a minor resistance line before the actual breakout, allowing for a tighter stop.
- **Volume Dry-Up (VDU):** During the final 1–2 weeks of the VCP, volume must drop to near-zero, signaling that there are no more sellers left to dump shares.
- **Hard Stop Discipline:** Minervini never risks more than 7–8% on any trade, but for swing traders, he often keeps the "average loss" near 5% to maximize the reward-to-risk ratio.

## Vincent Bruzzese: The "Market-Relative" Style

Summary: Bruzzese (Hari Seldon) focuses on Relative Strength/Weakness (RS/RW). He believes the individual stock's chart is only half the battle; the other half is the stock's performance relative to the SPY.

**Strategy Details**

- **The Wind at Your Back:** He only takes long swing trades when the SPY (Market) is above its 50-day and 200-day SMAs and showing bullish momentum.
- **RS/RW vs. SPY:** This is the core edge. If the SPY drops $2\%$ and your stock stays flat, that stock has massive "Institutional Support" and will likely rocket when SPY bounces.
- **The Heavy Daily Chart:** He looks for a Daily chart where all major moving averages (5, 50, 100, 200) are "stacked" and sloping upward.
- **Market Divergence:** He specifically hunts for "Higher Lows" in a stock during a period where the SPY is making "Lower Lows."

**Execution Signals**

- **The Breakout with RS:** A breakout is only valid if the stock is also hitting a new Relative Strength high versus the SPY at the same time.
- **The "Walkaway" Confirmation:** He evaluates entries by checking if the stock held its breakout levels even while the market pulled back intraday.
- **Volume Correlation:** He requires volume to be consistently high during the "up" days and significantly lower during the "down" days (institutional accumulation).
- **Sector Confirmation:** He cross-references the stock's sector (e.g., Tech or Energy) to ensure the entire group is showing RS versus the SPY.

## Chris Camillo: The "Social Arbitrage" Style

Summary: Camillo ignores charts and P/E ratios to focus on Information Imbalance. He buys based on real-world trends he spots on social media and in consumer behavior before Wall Street notices.

**Strategy Details**

- **The Social Tree:** He maps out narratives (e.g., "Weight Loss Drugs" or "AI PC") and looks for the specific companies that are mentioned most by actual consumers, not analysts.
- **Identifying the "Gap":** He looks for a "disconnect" where a product is going viral (TikTok/Google Trends) but the stock price hasn't reacted yet.
- **Cultural Momentum:** He trades the 20–60 day cycle of a trend, exiting once the "hype" becomes mainstream news and the "smart money" edge is gone.
- **High Conviction Betting:** Because he doesn't use technical stops, he only trades 1–3 setups a year but puts a significant percentage of his capital into them.

**Execution Signals**

- **The Google Trends Spike:** A sudden, vertical climb in search volume for a specific product or brand name.
- **The "Shelf-Emptying" Signal:** Real-world observation of product shortages or "Sold Out" signs that are not yet reflected in the company's financial guidance.
- **Social Sentiment Shift:** Using sentiment analysis tools or manual research to see if the "vibe" around a brand is changing from negative to positive.
- **Catalyst Anticipation:** Buying 30 days before a major cultural event (like a movie release or a product launch) that he expects will blow away expectations.

## Kristjan Kullamägi: The "Breakout & EP" Style

Summary: Kullamägi (Qullamaggie) looks for high-velocity "Momentum" stocks. He trades the "Meat of the Move" by buying explosive gap-ups or tight consolidations.

**Strategy Details**

- **Episodic Pivots (EP):** A massive gap up on volume driven by a fundamental surprise. This is the "birth" of a new multi-month trend.
- **High Tight Flags:** Finding stocks that have moved $100\%$ in 2 months and are now moving sideways. This is the most explosive swing pattern.
- **ADR (Average Daily Range):** He only trades stocks with high volatility (ADR > 4%), as these have the range to make 20–60% moves in your timeframe.
- **The 3-5 Day Rule:** He sells 1/3 to 1/2 of the position after 3–5 days of strength to lock in "income" and let the rest "ride" for the big swing.

**Execution Signals**

- **Opening Range Breakout (ORB):** He buys the high of the first 1, 5, or 60 minutes after the market opens on a breakout day.
- **10/20 EMA Trailing Stop:** For the "runner" portion of the trade, he stays in until the stock closes below the 10 or 20-day EMA.
- **The 10%+ Gap:** For an Episodic Pivot, the stock must gap up at least $10\%$ (preferably $15\%+$) on volume that is $5x$ or $10x$ the normal daily volume.
- **Tightness Near EMA:** He looks for the price to "surf" on top of the 10-day EMA without breaking it for several days before a new leg up.

## Oliver Kell: The "Price Cycle" Style

Summary: Kell (2020 Investing Champ) uses the interaction between the 10 and 20-day EMAs to navigate the different stages of a stock's growth cycle.

**Strategy Details**

- **The EMA Stack:** A "Perfect Trend" exists when the 10 EMA is above the 20 EMA, and both are above the 50 SMA.
- **The Price Cycle Stages:** He views stocks as moving through four stages: Wedge Pop (Entry), EMA Crossback (Pullback), Extension (Profit Taking), and Wedge Drop (Exit).
- **Relative Strength Line:** He insists that the stock's RS line must be hitting new highs before the price itself breaks out.
- **Volume Climax:** He watches for massive "blow-off" volume days when the stock is far from its EMAs as a signal that the swing is over.

**Execution Signals**

- **The Wedge Pop:** After a downtrend or consolidation, the price "pops" above both the 10 and 20-day EMAs simultaneously. This is his primary "Buy" signal.
- **The EMA Crossback:** A low-risk secondary entry where the stock pulls back to touch the 20-day EMA but does not close below it.
- **The Base n' Break:** A short, tight consolidation (1–3 weeks) that occurs while the stock is already trending above its EMAs.
- **Exhaustion Extension:** If a stock is $>20\%$ above its 10-day EMA, it is "extended," and he begins selling into that strength rather than buying more.

## The Unified 20–60 Day Swing Strategy

**1. The "What": High-Conviction Selection (Camillo + Minervini)**

- **The Social/Fundamental Trigger (Camillo):** Identify a stock with a "Cultural Catalyst"—a viral product, a supply chain shortage, or an earnings surprise that the market hasn't fully "priced in" yet.
- **The Trend Template (Minervini):** Only trade stocks in a Stage 2 Uptrend.
  - Price must be above a rising 200-day SMA.
  - The 50-day SMA must be above the 200-day SMA.

**2. The "When": Technical Entry (Velez + Minervini + Kullamägi)**

- **The VCP Consolidation (Minervini):** Look for a period of 2–6 weeks where the stock "tightens" (smaller price swings) on decreasing volume.
- **The Cheat Entry (Velez/Kullamägi):** Instead of waiting for a massive breakout, look for an Elephant Bar or a Bullish Reversal off the 10-day or 20-day EMA inside the consolidation.
- **The Trigger:** Buy as the price clears the high of a "tight" daily candle or an Episodic Pivot (gap up on high volume).

**3. The "How": Trade Management (Kell + Kullamägi)**

- **Initial Stop:** Set at the low of the "cheat" candle or 5-7% below entry (Minervini).
- **The 3-5 Day Rule (Kullamägi):** After 3 to 5 days of a strong move in your favor, sell 1/3 to 1/2 of your position. This "pays for the trade" and covers your initial risk.
- **The "Wealth" Runner (Kell):** Hold the remainder of the position as long as the 20-day EMA holds on a closing basis.
  - If the price closes below the 20-day EMA on heavy volume, the "swing" cycle is likely over (usually around the 40-60 day mark).

## The "Post-Earnings Drift" (PEAD)

Profit Mechanism: Institutional Under-reaction to Fundamental Shocks.
Academic research confirms that prices do not fully adjust to earnings surprises immediately but instead "drift" for up to 60 days.

**1. Profit Mechanism**

- Market Effect: Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD).
- Type: Information under-reaction and institutional liquidity friction.
- Timeframe: 40–60 days of persistent price movement.

**2. PM Behavior & Signals**

- **Standardized Unexpected Earnings (SUE):** The core driver is a surprise in the top decile of the market.
- **Signal (The Announcement):** A positive earnings surprise accompanied by an immediate "gap up" on high volume.
- **Behavioral Filter:** The drift is more pronounced in stocks with higher "information uncertainty" (lower analyst coverage or higher volatility).
- **Late-Day Signal:** The stock closes in the top 25% of its daily range on the day of the announcement, signaling that institutional buying is outweighing initial profit-taking.

**3. Outline Fitting Structures**

- **Normal Buy/Sell:** Long stock at the first day's close; trailing stop set to $2.0 \times$ the Average True Range (ATR).
- **Options Structure:** Bull Call Spread (Vertical).
  - Strike Selection: Buy At-The-Money (ATM) call and sell Out-of-The-Money (OTM) call.
  - Rationale: PEAD moves can be steady but slow; the vertical spread reduces the "Theta" cost of holding for 60 days.

**4. Build Strategy (Late-Day Workflow)**

- **Execution:** Between 3:30 PM and 4:00 PM on the day of an earnings beat, verify the stock is holding its "gap" levels.
- **Management:** Review the "drift" weekly; exit if the stock closes below its 20-day SMA or if the "drift" stalls for more than 5 consecutive days.

## The "Gamma-Induced Pinning" Effect

Profit Mechanism: Market Maker Hedging & Strike Magnetism.
Research on option expiration cycles shows that stock prices tend to "pin" toward strikes with the highest Open Interest as dealers hedge their Gamma exposure.

**1. Profit Mechanism**

- Market Effect: Stock Pinning.
- Type: Structural Alpha driven by Dealer Delta-Hedging.
- Timeframe: 5–20 days (specifically leading up to monthly options expiration).

**2. PM Behavior & Signals**

- **Max Pain/High Open Interest:** Identify the strike price with the largest combined Put and Call Open Interest.
- **Signal (The Magnetic Pull):** If a stock is within $2\text{--}3\%$ of a major strike price in the 10 days before expiration, it is likely to be "pulled" toward that strike.
- **Gamma Concentration:** High levels of dealer Gamma act as a stabilizing force, reducing volatility as the stock nears the strike.
- **Late-Day Signal:** Increasing volume at the specific strike price in the final 2 hours suggests active pinning is underway.

**3. Outline Fitting Structures**

- **Options Structure:** Iron Butterfly or Iron Condor.
  - Strike Selection: Center the structure on the "Pin" strike.
  - Rationale: Profit from the compression of volatility and the "pinning" of price at a specific level.

**4. Build Strategy (Late-Day Workflow)**

- **Identification:** Check "Open Interest" heatmaps 14 days before monthly OPEX.
- **Execution:** Enter the structure in the last hour of trading to avoid intraday volatility spikes.
- **Management:** Exit 1 day before expiration to avoid "Pin Risk" and assignment.

## The "Negative Volatility Risk Premium" (GJR-GARCH)

Profit Mechanism: Asymmetric Response to Negative Shocks.
Based on "Comparison of GARCH, EGARCH, GJR-GARCH, TGARCH," financial markets exhibit "leverage effects" where negative returns increase future volatility significantly more than positive returns of the same magnitude.

**1. Profit Mechanism**

- Market Effect: Volatility Asymmetry (Leverage Effect).
- Type: Structural risk premium driven by institutional hedging (panic).
- Timeframe: 20–40 days following a volatility spike.

**2. PM Behavior & Signals**

- **Asymmetric Shock:** A sharp price drop ($>2\sigma$) leads to a disproportionate spike in implied and realized volatility.
- **Signal (The GJR Trigger):** When a "Volatility Cluster" occurs following a down day, the probability of further downward momentum or elevated risk remains high for 20+ days.
- **Mean Reversion vs. Momentum:** In indices, this effect often leads to mean reversion (buying the fear); in individual stocks, it often leads to momentum to the downside.
- **Late-Day Signal:** If volatility (VIX or stock-specific IV) continues to rise into the close despite the price stabilizing, the risk premium is expanding.

**3. Outline Fitting Structures**

- **Options Structure:** Bear Put Spread or Long Put.
  - Rationale: Capture the volatility expansion and downward drift.
- **Managed Risk:** Use "Managed Volatility" rules to reduce position size as the GJR-GARCH model predicts higher risk.

**4. Build Strategy (Late-Day Workflow)**

- **Screening:** 3:00 PM—Identify stocks with high "Negative Volatility Persistence" (price down, vol up significantly).
- **Execution:** Enter between 3:45 PM and 4:00 PM to capture the "overnight" risk premium.

## The "Century Momentum" (Time-Series Persistence)

Profit Mechanism: Exploiting the 100-Year Persistence of Trend Following. Based on "Enduring Effect of Time-Series Momentum," the "trend is your friend" effect is not a market myth but a statistically robust anomaly that has persisted for nearly a century. It suggests that an asset's past 12-month return is a strong predictor of its next 1–3 month performance.

## The "Volatility Term Structure" (VTS) Slope Alpha

Summary: Research by Aurelio Vasquez shows that the shape of the implied volatility term structure (the difference between long-term and short-term IV) predicts future option and stock returns.

- **Profit Mechanism:** Volatility Over-reaction across Time Horizons.
- **The Signal:** Identify stocks where the 6-month Implied Volatility (IV) is significantly higher than the 1-month IV (a "Steep Slope").
- **The Effect:** Straddle portfolios with high VTS slopes significantly outperform. This suggests the market consistently overestimates long-term uncertainty relative to short-term realized risk.
